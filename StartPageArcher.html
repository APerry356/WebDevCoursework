THE WORLD AFTER

ARCHER START

Your lungs burn whislt you run through the remenants of the line. The infantry line meant to protect the archers has been torn apart by an enemy cavellry charge. Now they are chasing you. Your unit was in a poor state before this battle began, many had deserted into the surrounding forests, wanting to return home before this inevitable slaughter happend. Either from a sense of duty or from sheer stupidity you remained to fight, whatever it was that caused to remain with your unit, you've lost it now. The screaming erupts from your unit as the remaining archers drop their bows and arm themselves with anything they can. There is no order left, the officers have left, nowhere in sight. You can't help but feel that they've abandoned you. So much for noble knights fighting for the people.

You make it into the forrest but you do not stopping running, you cannot tell if the thumping you can hear is the beating of your heart or the drum of horse hoves chasing after you. Only once the sounds of battle fade behind you do you stop running and lean back against a tree, letting your breathing return to normal. The conforting sounds of the forrest do little to settle your troubled mind or to dispel the images of the battle that you are sure will plague you the rest of your life

Looking up at the sky you it dawns on you that you just deserted, you left your unit to die. You cannot return to your unit, if you're lucky they will hang you, you dare not think about what else they would do to you if they caught you. You remember a friend talking about a nearby village before he disappeard one quiet evening, a deserter like you. What other choice do you have? Besides you'll need supplies before you do anything else.
